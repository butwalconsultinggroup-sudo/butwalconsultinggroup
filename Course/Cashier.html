PRACTICAL CASH HANDLING TRAINING

Cashier

Learn how to manage cash flow, process payments, maintain register accuracy, and handle daily reconciliation with confidence.

Format Practical training
Focus Billing, cash control, reconciliation
Method Hands-on learning

Course content

14 modules - theory, POS practice, role-plays, and assessments

Module 1 Introduction to Cashiering and Hospitality
  • Overview of the hospitality and retail sectors in Nepal, including hotels, restaurants, supermarkets, and hospitals
  • Roles, duties, and responsibilities of a cashier
  • Importance of grooming, etiquette, and professional communication
  • Customer service basics and cultural sensitivity for Nepali and international guests
  • Work ethics, punctuality, and teamwork
Module 2 Currency, Pricing and VAT Regulations
  • Understanding the Nepalese rupee: denominations, currency symbols, and security features
  • Calculating prices inclusive or exclusive of 13% VAT, including zero-rated and exempt goods
  • Issuing manual bills and receipts, and recording VAT on invoices
  • Counting cash accurately and identifying counterfeit notes
  • Handling rounding rules and giving change
Module 3 POS Systems and Billing Operations
  • Introduction to POS billing software and hardware, including thermal printers, barcode scanners, and terminals
  • Setting up and operating cash registers and POS terminals
  • Applying discounts, service charges, and VAT
  • Printing, reprinting, and voiding bills, and managing corrections
  • End-of-day reports and cash closing procedures
  • Hands-on practice with local POS software
Module 4 Cash Handling and Transaction Processing
  • Cash handling cycle: receiving, processing, and disbursing funds
  • Types of transactions: cash, credit/debit cards, QR payments, mobile wallets, and cheques
  • Issuing refunds and processing returns, including common errors and how to avoid them
  • Petty-cash management and daily cash registers
  • Practical exercises: writing bills and receipts manually and counting cash accurately
Module 5 Balancing and Reconciliation
  • Opening cash drawers and assigning float amounts
  • Balancing cash drawers and reconciling POS totals at shift end
  • Handling cash overages and shortages with proper procedures and reporting
  • Preparing daily sales and cash reports and cross-checking with back-office records
  • Workshop: reconciliation and accuracy exercises
Module 6 Customer Service & Communicating Skills
  • Customer interaction techniques: greeting, polite conversation, and active listening
  • Using Nepali and English phrases for hospitality settings
  • Handling guest complaints and difficult customers professionally
  • Telephone manners and order-taking etiquette
  • Upselling and cross-selling techniques without pressuring the customer
  • Role-play: resolving disputes and processing transactions under pressure
Module 7 Digital Payment Methods & Technology
  • Overview of QR-based payment systems and digital wallets such as FonePay, eSewa, Khalti, and mobile banking
  • Nepal QR standardization and the importance of contactless payments
  • Merchant onboarding and KYC/KYM requirements, including verification and transaction monitoring
  • Accepting mobile payments on POS terminals and smartphones
  • Integrating POS systems with back-office databases
  • Security features such as PINs, OTPs, transaction limits, and digital receipts
  • Emerging technologies: digital wallets, contactless cards, and e-commerce checkout
  • Hands-on: generating and scanning QR codes and processing FonePay transactions
Module 8 Compliance, AML & Regulatory Requirements
  • Nepal’s AML/CFT framework and NRB supervision responsibilities
  • Customer due diligence and know-your-customer procedures
  • Know your merchant requirements for merchants using QR codes
  • Documentation and record-keeping for audit and regulatory compliance
  • Reporting suspicious transactions, threshold limits, and suspicious transaction reports
  • Consumer protection and data privacy laws
  • Understanding merchant category codes and NRB risk guidelines
Module 9 Fraud Detection and Prevention
  • Types of fraud in cashiering: counterfeit currency, card fraud, transaction laundering, and return fraud
  • Identifying counterfeit Nepalese banknotes through security threads and watermarks
  • Recognizing suspicious customer behavior and fraudulent cards
  • Internal controls to prevent employee theft and errors
  • Steps to take when fraud is suspected, including escalation procedures
  • Case studies and real-world fraud scenarios in Nepal
Module 10 Security and Risk Management
  • Security protocols for handling large cash amounts and high-risk transactions
  • Protecting customer and transaction data: PCI DSS basics
  • Personal safety and robbery prevention
  • Workplace health and safety, including hygiene measures
  • Risk management strategies and contingency planning
Module 11 Back-office Operations & Reporting
  • Record-keeping, data entry, and transaction tracking
  • Managing inventory and stock where cashiering involves storekeeping
  • Preparing daily, weekly, and monthly financial reports
  • Audit trails and documentation for tax and regulatory purposes
  • Using technology to automate back-office tasks
  • Coordinating with accounting and management teams through workflow integration
Module 12 Storekeeping & Inventory Management
  • Receiving goods, stock rotation, and labeling
  • Use of barcode scanners and inventory software
  • Stock counting, re-ordering, and expiry management
  • Maintaining a clean and organized storage area
  • Preparing goods return notes and damage reports
Module 13 Soft Skills, Grooming and Workplace Etiquette
  • Professional appearance and grooming standards
  • Time management, stress management, and adaptability
  • Teamwork and cooperation with colleagues and supervisors
  • Ethics, integrity, and confidentiality
Module 14 Job Preparation and Career Development
  • Writing a CV tailored to cashier or hospitality roles
  • Preparing for interviews and common cashier interview questions
  • Understanding work culture abroad and adaptation tips
  • Opportunities for advancement such as senior cashier, supervisor, and bank teller
